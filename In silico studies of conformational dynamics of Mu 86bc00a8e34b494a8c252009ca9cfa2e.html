Goal

Prior Knowledge

  • Can signal through arrestin and this contributes to adverse effects such as antinociceptive tolerance, physical dependence, and respiratory suppression, and constipation.
  • 3 conformational states — inactive, intermediate, and active — exist for most GPCRs
  • Biased towards active when it is bound to agonist and switch to inactive when it is bound to antagonist.
  • Biased towards intemediate when not bound to agonist or inverse agonist. so apo.
  • Inactivation and activation occur over ms or longer.

Takeaway

  • In silico studies of conformational dynamics of Mu opioid receptor performed using gaussian accelerated molecular dynamics
    • Functionally key residues
      • Active
        • Electrostatic: Tyr148, Asp147,
        • Van der waals: Ile322, Ile 144, Val 236, Met151, Val300, Ile296
        • H-bonding: Tyr 326
      • Inactive
        • Electrostatic: Asp147, Tyr148, Lys233, Lys303,
        • Van der waals: Val236, Val300, Met152, Ile296, Ile322, Trp293, Tyr326
        • H-bonding: Asp147, Tyr148
      • Critical Residues
        • E/DRY motif is responsible for activation of the GPCR
        • Key residues for binding are:
          • Asp D 147 His 297 Y 326
